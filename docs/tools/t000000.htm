These kinds of work items are lately kept in TODO sections of text files, such as [tools.txt](tools.txt).  Those items are removed as they are resolved and the development history, with all of the warts and barnacles is lost to those who do not have access to the GitHub repository (or a clone) and the "blame" history.  In previous web-based folios, the job jar is preserved, with resolved items moved to the end of the list.
 
Either form is a personal-memory extension.  The Diary & Job Jar form has history independent of the source-code version management. And there is no "blame" meme.  Job Jars are reviewable without having repository access, whether directly or via a clone.  I rather missed that.  Using Markdown in that fashion is not so pleasant as when I had FrontPage to make these.
 
One appeal that Job Jars have is that not every entry and action requires treatment as an additional version.  The progression is reflected in the material and that is taken as sufficient, in contrast with TODOs being deleted as part of Git change commits.

Now that I have established that FrontPage can make GitHub Pages perfectly well, thank you, I am heartened to have continuity once again.  There is much curation required to complete the repurposing here, but much of that is accomplished with the revamping of the Construction Material and its templates.

Side note: The materials here have been scavenged from nfoCentrale/nfoWare, although the graphical choice here for logos and icons is from nfoWorks.  That will be sorted out.
